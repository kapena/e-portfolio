The Secret Of Dreaming

Digital Animation

The Secret of Dreaming is a short digital animation that tells the story of how the Dreaming spirit passed the spirit of Creation on throughout the continent of Australia. By digitally recreating Jim Poulter’s children’s book, The Secret of Dreaming,  I was able to honor The Dreaming's major significance to Aboriginal's of Australian and their culture.

The Concept

Concepting is a significant and crucial process in creating films. For the Secret Of Dreaming, I was looking at obtaining a visual style that resembled Aboriginal cave art and raw Australian landscape textures. Each of the concepts you see underneath played a major role in creating The Secret Of Dreaming's unique visual style.

Animatic

The animatic is a preliminary version of a The Secret Of Dreaming. By shooting successive drawings of the storyboard, I was able to put the visual concepts directly into motion and calculate timing for each scene. You'll notice in the animatic how some rough ideas are put to use. The animatic is a rough version of the actual film. Not all scenes were are included.

The Design

The finalized visual design for The Secret Of Dreaming consists of textures and colors that take inspiration from the raw Australian landscapes. Each character was hand drawn then digitally enhanced to fit inside their habitat. A combination of Digital effects, 3D animation, 2D hand-drawn characters and textured digital environments create the feeling of the raw, untamed bush of Australia.

Sound Design

Achieving the sound design for the Secret Of Dreaming was made possible by collaborating with Jack Charles for the narration and Greg Macdonald for the sound editing and production. Jack Charles is an Australian Aboriginal actor, musician, potter, and Aboriginal elder from Melbourne, Australia.

Outcome

The Secret Of Dreaming illustrates an ancient story of how the Aboriginals of Australia saw life through the Dreaming. 60,000 years later we can still learn from The Dreaming that earth is a precious planet, and it's our duty as humans to protect it.
